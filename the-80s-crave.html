The 80s Crave

Nintendo released the console Entertainment System or NES in 1983 with eight-bit graphics, colors, sound and gameplay. Bits stand for binary digits and the amount represents how much a system can process and represent information.On top of the console upgrade, newer higher quality games also grabbed consumers attention. These games included, Super Mario Bros, and Legend of Zelda which are still played today.

In 1988 Nintendo with leading designer,Gunpei Yokoi would release the Game & Watch range. Yokoi made his start as a maintenance engineer before his knack for making homemade toys would impress the President of Nintendo when he visited Yokoi’s factory. Yokoi is considered a legend in the console industry, creating the honorable Game Boy a year later in 1989. Game Boy was a 8-bit CPU build that had 64-Kbit static RAM and monochrome display and battery life that was prolonged AA batteries
