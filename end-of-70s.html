End of 70s

Atari, would develop the video computer system known as Atari 2600, officiating what is known as the second generation of consoles in 1977 and the console would have joysticks and interchangeable game cartridges. Cartridges are physical storage media essentially containing the game data and able to be inserted into consoles for play.

Microvision, the first handheld console to use interchangeable cartridges was created in 1979 by Milton Bradley, it was a 8-bit device with LCD screen. However, it was a failure due to its technical problems such as screen quality and keypad sensitivity.
