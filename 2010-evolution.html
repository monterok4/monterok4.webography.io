2010 Evolution

Nintendo had begun to lose it's footing in the console world, but they would attempt to step back into the ring with a alright fight when they released the Wii U in 2012. It lost, the Wii U was not a success marketwise, however Nintendo would later revisit its structure which featured a mix of handheld console and standard console in the future. Meanwhile, their current handheld console device, the Nintendo 3DS (2011) would go on to have great success keeping up with the lines dual screen but adding into newer visual aspects. Playstation and Xbox continued with their releases as well. The PS 4 would revolutionize mobile and console gaming in a collaborative way with it allowing players the ability to connect remotes to their IOS or Android, PC or Mac devices with compatible Playstation games. The Xbox One also managed to meet the current trends of the time by allowing players to record or take pictures and share from gameplay or livestream directly on services including Twitch. The console also featured the Kinect which was essentially a camera and microphone device designed to pick up on users voice and movement in relation to the console. Later in 2017, as mentioned before, Nintendo would rework their Wii U to the Nintendo Switch. The console would be the only console and 1st that allowed for both television and handheld gaming. Further releases include the Xbox One X which offered 4K and UHD visuals for the 1st time win entire video game console history and the PS5 which pledged to move forward in eco-friendliness.
